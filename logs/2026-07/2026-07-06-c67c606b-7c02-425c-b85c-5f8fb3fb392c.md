𝗧𝗲𝘀𝘁-𝗱𝗿𝗶𝘃𝗲𝗻 𝗱𝗲𝘃𝗲𝗹𝗼𝗽𝗺𝗲𝗻𝘁 𝗰𝗵𝗮𝗻𝗴𝗲𝗱 𝗵𝗼𝘄 𝘄𝗲 𝘄𝗿𝗶𝘁𝗲 𝘀𝗼𝗳𝘁𝘄𝗮𝗿𝗲.

Write the test first. Watch it fail. Make it pass.

Same principle applies to AI agents — but almost nobody does it.

Most teams start with the model. They iterate on prompts, chain-of-thought, RAG pipelines. Then they wonder why the agent drifts. Why it works today and breaks tomorrow.

The fix: benchmark first.

Define the verifiable outcome BEFORE you write a single line of agent logic.

What's the task? What does "done" look like? How do you measure it?

The benchmark IS your reward function. Your agent optimizes toward it. Every iteration moves the needle.

This is how you build self-evolving agents — not by hoping they figure it out, but by giving them a clear target and letting them chase it.

TDD gave us reliable software. Benchmark-first gives us reliable AI.

Are you building your agent around a benchmark — or just hoping it works?

A good benchmark answers three questions:

1. **What's the task?** (specific, not vague)
2. **What does "done" look like?** (verifiable outcome)
3. **How do you measure it?** (quantifiable metric)

Most teams skip this. They build the agent first, then scramble to figure out if it's working.

Flip it. Write the benchmark BEFORE a single line of code. Make it pass. Then make the agent chase it.

The benchmark IS your reward function. Every iteration moves the needle. Every failure teaches the system.

This is TDD for AI. Test first. Agent second.
