They cut their riskiest reconciliation job in half — by making AI agents LESS autonomous.

Not more.
Less.

Everyone's chasing "fully autonomous agents."
Morgan Stanley went the opposite direction.
Fixed rules. Human sign-off on every call. Fewer probabilistic decisions.

Result?
The job that used to break their team is now reliable.

Here's the insight most teams miss:
Autonomy is a feature for demos.
Control is a feature for production.

The companies actually shipping AI agents aren't building self-driving everything.
They're building systems that know WHEN to ask for permission.

If your AI agent can't explain what it's about to do before it does it —
you're not ready for production.
You're ready for a demo.

What's the most fragile process in your business that could use an agent with guardrails?
