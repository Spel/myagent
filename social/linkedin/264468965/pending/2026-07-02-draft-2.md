Z.ai just launched ZCode.
Now you've got Cursor. Claude Code. GitHub Copilot. ZCode.
All fighting for your terminal.

Good news for developers.
Bad news for anyone who thought this space was settled.

Here's what nobody's talking about:
Every new AI coding tool = another integration to manage.
Another context window to pay for.
Another agent that "knows" your codebase... separately.

The real opportunity isn't picking ONE tool.
It's building infrastructure that lets you swap them.

BYOK (bring your own key) is becoming table stakes.
Multi-model orchestration is next.
If your workflow is locked to one vendor, you're not future-proof — you're vendor-hostage.

The winners won't be the teams with the best AI.
They'll be the teams who can swap AIs without breaking everything.

How many AI tools is too many before you lose control?
