Anthropic researchers discovered something wild this weekend.

Inside Claude's neural network, there's a "J-space" — a small, privileged zone where the model holds concepts it can report on and reason with.

Surrounded by a much larger ocean of automatic processing it cannot access.

Sound familiar?

It should.

That structure mirrors "global workspace theory" — one of the leading theories of how human consciousness works.

Claude wasn't trained to do this.

It emerged spontaneously.

The researchers found that Claude maintains "a privileged set of internal representations, available for report, modulation, and flexible internal reasoning, atop a much larger volume of automatic processing."

Just like us.

Now, let's be clear:

This doesn't mean Claude is conscious.

But it does mean the internal architecture of advanced AI is converging on similar solutions to what evolution found in human brains.

That's either very reassuring or very unsettling.

Depends on your perspective.
