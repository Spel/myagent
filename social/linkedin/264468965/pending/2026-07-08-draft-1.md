𝗧𝗵𝗲 𝗯𝗶𝗴𝗴𝗲𝘀𝘁 𝗔𝗜 𝘀𝘁𝗼𝗿𝘆 𝗻𝗼𝗯𝗼𝗱𝘆'𝘀 𝘁𝗮𝗹𝗸𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗮𝗯𝗼𝘂𝘁:

Most AI agent users aren't coding.

Anthropic just published data from 1.2 million Claude Cowork sessions.

The overwhelming majority of what people do has nothing to do with writing software.

This matters more than you think.

The AI agent narrative has been dominated by developer tools.

Coding assistants.

Terminal workflows.

Technical users.

But the real market?

It's knowledge workers who never open a terminal.

Anthropic just expanded Cowork to mobile and web.

Tasks start on a laptop, continue autonomously in the background, get reviewed from a phone.

"Your work goes everywhere with you, and keeps going without you."

That's the shift.

AI agents aren't just for engineers anymore.

They're for everyone who works with information.

Which is basically everyone.

If your AI strategy is still focused on coding productivity, you're missing the bigger picture.

The real opportunity is automating knowledge work itself.

What non-coding workflow would you automate first?
