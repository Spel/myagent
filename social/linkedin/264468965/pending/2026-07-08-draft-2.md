New data from Box's State of AI in the Enterprise report:

Last year: 8% of companies called themselves "advanced" or "leading edge" in AI.

This year: 64%.

That's an 8x jump in 12 months.

Meanwhile, companies calling themselves "early stage" or "not started" collapsed from 53% to 9%.

Here's the real story:

Half of leading-edge companies reported AI ROI above 25%.

Only 11% of early-stage companies hit that mark.

The difference isn't technology.

It's execution.

Olivia Nottebohm, Box COO: "We've moved from standalone experimentation into systematized, integrated agentic operations, agents that are in production and can be used in a repeatable manner."

That's where the impact comes from.

Not pilots.

Not experiments.

Production systems that work repeatably.

80% of organizations saw notable ROI within 6 months of project approval.

The window to catch up is still open.

But it's closing fast.

Where does your organization land — leader or laggard?
