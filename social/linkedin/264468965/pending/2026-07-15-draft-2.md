𝗖𝗵𝗲𝗮𝗽𝗲𝗿 𝗺𝗼𝗱𝗲𝗹𝘀 𝗱𝗼𝗻'𝘁 𝗳𝗶𝘅 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝟭𝟬𝟬𝘅 𝗽𝗿𝗼𝗯𝗹𝗲𝗺.

DeepSeek just cut prices 75%.

You'd think that's great news for margins.

It's not.

Here's why.

A chatbot turns one user question into one model call.

An agent turns it into a chain:

Planning. Retrieval. Tool use. Verification. Summarization. Follow-up.

The user sees one answer.

You pay for the loop.

That's the 100x problem:

The same user-visible request can cost 100x more as an agentic workflow than as a chatbot.

Cheaper models help.

But they don't fix an architecture that turns one prompt into dozens of billable operations.

This is the hidden cost of the agent revolution everyone's ignoring.

For 20 years, software economics was simple: infra gets cheaper, apps get more capable.

AI was supposed to follow.

It didn't.

Your agent stack is burning tokens faster than prices are dropping.

The solution isn't cheaper models.

It's smarter orchestration.

What's your agent architecture doing to control the loop?
