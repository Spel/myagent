That's the evaluation gap.

Agents are gaining autonomy faster than companies can verify them.

The numbers:

- 50% of enterprises deployed an AI agent that passed internal evals... and still caused a customer-facing failure
- 25% had it happen more than once
- 66% are deploying agents without human review (or planning to within 12 months)
- Only 5% fully trust their automated evaluations

That's the mismatch.

Traditional software testing asks: "Does input X produce output Y?"

Agent testing asks: "Did the agent make good decisions across an unpredictable sequence of steps, tool calls, and state changes?"

Those are completely different problems.

The agent can make several individually plausible decisions that combine into a disaster.

And you won't catch it with unit tests.

The next year will be a retrofit cycle.

Enterprises are shipping agents first.

Then scrambling to build the governance layers after.

Are you evaluating your agents for what they actually do?

Or just for what they output?
