Here's the fix.

Most teams default to the most expensive model for every task.

Why? Because it's easier.

But that's like hiring a senior architect to assemble IKEA furniture.

Technically correct. Financially insane.

ACRouter just open-sourced a better approach.

It treats model routing as a dynamic agent — not a static classification problem.

It tracks which models succeed at which tasks.

It learns from failures.

It routes prompts to the right model automatically.

The result?

2.6x cheaper than defaulting to premium models.

Without sacrificing quality.

This matters more than you think.

Single-model setups are useful for experiments.

But they're detrimental when scaling AI applications.

Smart routing maps tasks to cheaper, faster models when possible.

It reserves expensive frontier models for complex reasoning.

Your AI stack should be self-optimizing.

Not hardcoded.
