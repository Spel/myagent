Let that sink in.

50% of companies have deployed an AI agent that passed internal evaluations.

And still caused a customer-facing failure.

One in four — more than once.

Here's the scary part:

66% of enterprises already permit some production deployment without human review.

Or are building systems to do so within 12 months.

But only 5% fully trust the automated evaluations making those release decisions.

That's the evaluation gap.

Agents are gaining autonomy faster than companies can verify them.

Traditional software testing asks: "Does this input produce the expected output?"

Agent testing is harder.

The system chooses its own steps.

Calls tools.

Retrieves data.

Alters state.

Responds differently from one run to the next.

You're not testing code anymore.

You're testing behavior.

And most teams aren't equipped for that.

The next year will be a retrofit cycle.

Budgets shifting toward systems that make agents governable and dependable.

Are you shipping agents? Or are you shipping safely?
