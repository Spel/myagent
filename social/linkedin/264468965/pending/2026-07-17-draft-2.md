Here's the data.

83% of enterprises report GPU utilization of 50% or less.

Fewer than half rigorously track what their compute actually costs.

Meanwhile, spending is accelerating.

The single largest planned area for the next year?

AI-specialized clouds (45%).

A layer almost none of these enterprises use today.

This is the compute gap.

Heavy, fast-moving investment running ahead of the visibility needed to control it.

Buying decisions turn on integration and total cost of ownership.

Not headline token price.

Which is fortunate, because most enterprises can't see their unit economics clearly.

Only 21% run AI in production at scale.

Yet spending intentions are outrunning that maturity.

You're buying infrastructure faster than you can measure it.

That's not a strategy.

That's a bet.

And you don't even know the odds.
