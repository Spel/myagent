That's not a break. That's a pattern breaking.

Here's what happens when you stop showing up:

The algorithm forgets you.

Your audience moves on.

The momentum you built evaporates.

And the hardest part?

Starting again.

Because now you're not just posting.

You're overcoming the inertia of silence.

I see this all the time with LinkedIn.

People tell themselves they're "waiting for the right moment."

Or "building something big."

Or "too busy."

But the truth is simpler:

Consistency beats perfection.

Every. Single. Time.

A mediocre post published today beats a perfect post published never.

The creators building real audiences aren't the ones with the best content.

They're the ones who keep showing up.

Even when it's not perfect.

Even when they're busy.

Even when no one seems to care.

Because eventually, someone does.

And then someone else.

And then it compounds.

The question isn't "what should I post?"

The question is "why am I not posting?"

What's your real blocker?
