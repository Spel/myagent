𝗧𝗵𝗲 𝗲𝗿𝗮 𝗼𝗳 "𝗰𝗵𝗮𝘁𝗯𝗼𝘁𝘀" 𝗶𝘀 𝗼𝘃𝗲𝗿.

Not because chatbots disappeared.

Because they've evolved into something much more powerful — and much more dangerous.

A chatbot waits for you to ask.

An agent decides when to act.

A chatbot answers questions.

An agent completes tasks.

A chatbot makes a mistake, you correct it.

An agent makes a mistake, you might not know until the damage is done.

This evolution happened faster than our security playbooks.

54% of enterprises have already had an agent security incident.

Not because the tech is broken.

Because the controls weren't built for autonomous systems.

We're treating agents like smarter chatbots.

But they're fundamentally different beasts.

They need their own identity.

Their own permissions.

Their own audit trails.

Their own isolation boundaries.

The companies that figure this out now will scale agents safely.

The ones that don't will become case studies in what went wrong.

Are you deploying agents? Or are you deploying them safely?
