𝗔𝘀𝗸 𝘆𝗼𝘂𝗿 𝗰𝗵𝗶𝗲𝗳 𝗰𝗹𝗼𝘂𝗱 𝗮𝗿𝗰𝗵𝗶𝘁𝗲𝗰𝘁 𝘁𝗵𝗶𝘀 𝗾𝘂𝗲𝘀𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻:

"What's our cost per AI inference?"

If they can answer immediately, you're in the minority.

83% of GPUs run at 50% utilization or less.

44% rigorously track compute costs.

21% run AI in production at scale.

The spending is real. The visibility isn't.

You're not optimizing for cost.

You're optimizing for speed.

That works until the CFO asks for ROI.

Then you need numbers you probably don't have.

Start here:

1. Total monthly AI compute spend
2. GPU utilization rate
3. Cost per inference / per token / per task
4. Revenue or savings attributed to AI

If you have #1 but not #4, you're not running an AI strategy.

You're running a pilot with an expanding budget.

What would your CFO say if asked for AI ROI today?
