𝗬𝗼𝘂𝗿 𝗔𝗜 𝗽𝗶𝗹𝗼𝘁 𝗱𝗶𝗱𝗻'𝘁 𝗳𝗮𝗶𝗹 𝗯𝗲𝗰𝗮𝘂𝘀𝗲 𝗼𝗳 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗺𝗼𝗱𝗲𝗹.

It failed because your data pipeline is broken.

Here's what I keep seeing: Enterprise spends months building a RAG architecture. Vector database, embeddings, retrieval layer — the works. Then the AI hallucinates, exposes wrong context, or just... underwhelms.

The instinct? Blame the model. Tune the prompts. Buy a better embedding engine.

The real problem? Garbage in, garbage out.

If your source data has schema drift, duplicate records, and conflicting states — your vector store inherits that chaos. No amount of semantic reranking fixes a broken foundation.

The "cleanup trap" is believing you can pipe messy legacy data into an LLM and patch it at retrieval.

You can't.

Winning teams validate BEFORE AI sees the data. Schema checks at ingestion. Statistical profiling for drift. Anomaly detection that pauses the pipeline before corrupted metadata spreads.

Stop asking RAG to fix bad data. Fix the data first.
