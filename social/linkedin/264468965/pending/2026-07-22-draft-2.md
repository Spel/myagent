Data centers will consume 4x more electricity by 2035.

That's not a climate headline. That's a margin compression warning.

Here's the math:

AI runs on compute. Compute runs on electricity. Electricity costs money.

The companies building AI infrastructure are passing those costs downstream. Every query, every inference, every agent task — it all burns kilowatts.

Right now, everyone's focused on model selection and prompt engineering.

Wait until they see the infrastructure bill.

Winners will architect for efficiency from day one:
- Smaller, task-specific models over bloated generalists
- Edge deployment where it makes sense
- Caching strategies that reduce redundant calls

Losers will build cool demos and bleed cash at scale.

Are you building for efficiency — or just building?
